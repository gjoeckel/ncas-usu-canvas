Welcome to Core Accessibility Skills

Core Accessibility Skills is a series of mini-modules offered by the National Center on Accessible Digital Educational Materials & Instruction (NCADEMI or “n-cademy”). Making digital instructional content accessible means ensuring that learners with disabilities can perceive, navigate, and engage with learning materials. These nine Core Accessibility Skills help educators create more accessible digital documents, videos, and images.

Intended Users

This resource can be used by:

  • Individual educators who want to strengthen their accessibility skills.
  • Education agencies—such as states or school districts—interested in offering large-scale professional learning.
  • Educator preparation programs seeking to integrate accessibility practices into general and special education curricula.

While NCADEMI does not currently provide credit for module completion, we are working toward this. Credit-bearing agencies and institutions are free to use and adapt this resource under the terms of the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International License.

Completing Core Accessibility Skills

Core Accessibility Skills is hosted on Utah State University’s Canvas platform. You can explore all module content without signing in. There are links to nine skills on the Core Skills page. Each mini-module follows a consistent layout:

  • Why the skill matters
  • Learning objectives
  • Tips for success
  • A use-case example
  • A shareable resource
  • A quiz to check your understanding with unlimited attempts
    • To take quizzes:
      • Create a free USU Canvas account
      • Log in to USU Canvas using the link on the navigation bar

You should plan for 10 - 20 minutes to complete each mini-module.

Tracking your Progress

After you created a free USU Canvas account and have logged in, you will be able to track your progress.

Core Skills

On the Core Skills page, each mini-module link will display a quiz status in its upper-right corner:

There are three statuses:

  1. Pending: Quiz not started
  2. Active: Quiz taken
  3. Done: Quiz completed with score of 100%

Progress page

You will also see a new link on the navigation bar to the Progress page:

The table on this page has three columns:

  1. Name: The name of the quiz and a link back to the quiz’s page.
  2. Status: The quiz’s current status of Pending, Active, or Done.
  3. Score: Your highest score and the total points possible (e.g., 3/3).
